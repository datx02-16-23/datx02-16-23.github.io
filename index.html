Progress (updated weekly)

  • Visual prototyping Week 4

    This week, we've finished the planning report but the more interesting thing is that we started creating visual prototypes of what we believe we want to do. The intermittent language of the final product will be created as a analysis of the visual prototypes and how a language could describe them.

  • Planning, planning, planning Week 3

    This weeks focus has been on creating a good planning report. We have done some meetings and a workshop leading up to a somewhat finished planning report, what's left is some spellchecking and creating LaTeX files.

    We have started setting up Scrum model, where we are using Waffle.io as technical support and Dennis is our Scrum Master. We are going to have an initial backlog workshop when the planning report is done.

  • "Dream on while you can" Week 2

    This week we thought we were somewhat done with our vison. In no substantial way was this true. After our supervisor meeting, he gave us some well needed insights that it's better to do a cool thing, breaking new ground, than just to focus on what is deliverable as soon and easy as possible.

    Our vision has changed, now we are targeting in making a good intermediate representation of a visualization, instead of hooking some Java annotations to a drawing function. This intermediate representation will represent a "steppable" visualization, instead of being directly hooked to a programming language environment.

  • A bit on how we workWeek 1

    As this first week is over we have set up aids and systems on how to collaborate. We've agreed on trying to have two standard meetings each weeks, usually one at the start and one in the end of each week. Time reports are done through a collaborative spreadsheet . We will use ShareLaTeX for joint work on the final report and GitHub for working on code.

    As for roles in the project we have a project leader, Ivar, and an evaluator, Jonathan. The project leader role is responsible for driving the project forward and the evaluator is responsible making sure what's done is done and of good standard. The roles may rotate between project members throughout the project.

  • First (official) week of the project! Week 1

    Inbetween our first lectures we've had two meetings. In our tuesday meeting we agreed on methods of communications, ways to share resources and meeting templates. We started working on our vision and made a draft of our group contract.

    In our second meeting, we started doing status reports, a recurring point on each meeting onward. We also, by some of the insights of lectures this week, decided to assign roles. Ivar is assigned project leader until further notice and Jonathan is assigned to evaluate the project process through this part of the project.

    We have limited the scope of the project a bit by deciding on some restrictions. The focus of our project should be tracking changes to data structures and enforcing a 2D visualisation of simpler data structures, annotated by a custom markup. The target of our project will be Java and simpler data structures, but should be easy to extend to other languages and data structures in the future.

    Our priority for the next week is finishing a draft for the project planning report.

  • A startup meeting Week 0

    At 2015-12-17 we had our first meeting. The meeting focused on deliverables, methods of communication and some brief pointers on project management. Also, we got some needed pointers of what our project proposal lacks. The project proposal is unclear and our bibliograpy is severely lacking. We got tasked to produce a web site containing contact information, project diary and minutes.
